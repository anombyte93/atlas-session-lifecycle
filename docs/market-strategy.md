## The "Soul Purpose" Concept as Marketing

### Why "Soul Purpose" Works

Current project management uses:
- Jira tickets (task-level)
- GitHub issues (code-level)
- Roadmaps (feature-level)

**No one uses goal-level tracking at the Claude Code layer.**

"Soul purpose" fills that gap:

> **Soul Purpose** = The one thing this project exists to achieve.
>
> Not what you're building THIS SPRINT. Not what you're fixing TODAY.
> What the project EXISTS FOR.

### Examples (Concrete)

**Example 1: Auth Refactor**
- Jira tickets: "Implement OAuth2", "Add rate limiting", "Write tests"
- Soul purpose: "Migrate legacy auth to industry-standard OAuth2 without breaking production"

**Example 2: Feature Delivery**
- GitHub issues: "Add search API", "Build search UI", "Index documents", "Test performance"
- Soul purpose: "Ship a fast, user-friendly search experience that reduces time-to-value by 40%"

**Example 3: Bug Fixes**
- Tickets: "Fix memory leak", "Optimize query", "Update dependencies"
- Soul purpose: "Stabilize the database layer for 99.9% uptime"

### Why This Resonates with Developers

1. **Clarity**: One sentence defines "done"
2. **Autonomy**: Claude Code can work toward a clear objective
3. **Verification**: Progress can be measured against the purpose
4. **Motivation**: Developers know why they're doing the work

This is why the $30k contract mention is powerful:
> "I just delivered on a $30,000 contract thanks to Claude Code"

Soul purpose tracking is why that delivery was possible (and verifiable).

## Pricing Model Rationale

### Why $12/month for Pro?
- Breakeven for solopreneur at 1-2 paid projects/month
- Low enough to be "try-worthy"
- High enough to attract serious users
- Annual discount ($120/year) rewards committed users

### Why Custom Enterprise?
- Complex requirements (Jira sync, SAML, custom rules)
- High setup cost for us
- Value is multi-disciplinary (project ops, QA, leadership visibility)
- Market will bear $500-2k/month for larger teams

### Why Free Forever?
- Cornering the market (barriers to entry are bad for adoption)
- Community goodwill (open source projects are trusted)
- Freemium businesses have higher lifetime value per user
- Soloists and students are future power users and advocates
